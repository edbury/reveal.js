Goodbye, Hammer

A lovestory about design ops

Today

Hammer runs the show.

  • Our prototype markup and sass are both compiled by Hammer.
  • It generates compiled prototypes on-demand for off-team testing / investigation.
  • Hammer is straightforward and allowed us hit the ground running.

The Problems

(Not Quite) 99 of Them

  • Hammer is outdated and stagnant.
  • Hammer is a black box.
  • Hammer is limited.
  • Hammer is a bad hand-off.

The Solution

Enter Catapult, Assemble, and Camelot

  • Catapult - our new prototyping repo, with each product proto being built out by Grunt
  • Assemble - a static site generator to handle our templates and data (the Hammer killer)
  • Camelot - a separate repo for our stylesheets, pulled in as a dependency

The Return

Winning / Tiger Blood

  • Separating out our Sass as an npm package allows engineering to include it as a dependency, instead of merging stylesheet changes manually
  • We control our dependencies, libraries, and compilers: no more Sass Stoneage
  • Assemble/Handlebars templates allow us to write markup in reusable chunks, making updates and iterations much more sane
  • Prototypes can be served anywhere
